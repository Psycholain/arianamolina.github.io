My Guiding Principles

Inclusion & Diversity

As someone with a multicultural background, I place strong importance on building inclusive environments where diverse perspectives are valued. I aim to ensure that data-driven decisions reflect the needs of all users — including those who are often underrepresented.

Continuous Learning

I see learning as an ongoing process. In a rapidly evolving field, I actively seek to expand my knowledge — whether through new technologies, research methods, or interdisciplinary perspectives.

Curiosity

Curiosity drives my work. I constantly question assumptions, explore patterns, and look beyond the obvious to better understand behaviors and uncover meaningful insights.

Trust & Integrity

I believe that trust is essential in both data and collaboration. I strive to ensure that my analyses are transparent, reliable, and ethically grounded, while building strong and respectful relationships with stakeholders.

About me

Some elements representing different aspects of my personality.

I'm Ariana Molina, a UX Researcher and Data Analyst with a multicultural background — born in Ecuador, and with 3 different nationalities, I have lived and worked across 5 different countries, which has given me a unique ability to understand diverse perspectives and adapt quickly to new environments.

My academic journey led me to a Master's degree in Applied Economics at the University of Neuchâtel, where I developed a strong foundation in statistical modeling, data analysis and behavioral economics.

As a neurodivergent person, I experience the world with an intensity and curiosity that I consider one of my greatest strengths. I'm naturally wired to seek novelty, make unexpected connections between ideas, and dive deep into complex problems until I find meaningful answers. This is not a limitation — it's the engine behind my creativity, my drive to keep learning, and my ability to bring alternative perspectives to every project I work on.

I deeply value inclusion and equal representation — not just as professional values, but as lived experience. When I'm not analyzing data or designing research studies, you'll find me playing chess, exploring via ferratas, or cooking dishes from everywhere around the world just for fun.
